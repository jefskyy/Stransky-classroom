Same Device, Different Purpose

A heat engine, refrigerator, and heat pump are identified partly by the directions of heat and work. A refrigerator and heat pump can describe the same physical cycle, but their performance metrics differ because the desired result differs. For a heat engine, the desired result is net work output. For a refrigerator, it is heat removed from the low-temperature space. For a heat pump, it is heat delivered to the high-temperature space. In this activity, QH, QL, and W are positive magnitudes; the arrows show energy direction.

Performance Metric Comparison

You have completed at least one heat-engine, refrigerator, and heat-pump interpretation.

Device Desired result Required input Metric
Heat engine Wnet,out QH Thermal efficiency = Wnet,out / QH
Refrigerator QL Wnet,in COPR = QL / Wnet,in
Heat pump QH Wnet,in COPHP = QH / Wnet,in

Key interpretation: COP can exceed 1 because the desired heat transfer includes both the heat moved from the low-temperature region and the supplied work. COP is not the fraction of one input converted into one output, so it is not bounded in the same way as heat-engine efficiency.
